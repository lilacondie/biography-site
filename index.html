Billy Corgan

One of the biggest influences in alternative rock.

Who Is Billy Corgan?

Originally from Chicago, William Patrick Corgan Jr., better known as Billy Corgan, is best known for being the lead singer, songwriter, and guitarist of The Smashing Pumpkins. The Smashing Pumpkins are considered to be one of the most influential alternative rock bands of the 1990s. Corgan’s songwriting and skills as a guitarist are some of the aspects that made the Smashing Pumpkins so popular. With the Smashing Pumpkins, he has won two Grammys, and has also had a successful solo career. He is also the owner and promoter of the National Wrestling Alliance and owner of Madame Zuzu’s, a restaurant located in Chicago.

Career Timeline

Formative Years

After meeting guitarist James Iha in 1988, Corgan and Iha begin to write songs together and perform as a duo. Corgan then meets bass player D’arcy Wretzky and recruits her for the band. With the band still in need of a drummer, Jimmy Chamberlin joins. The Smashing Pumpkins are officially formed with Corgan being the frontman. The group release their first single “I Am One” in 1990, then release their debut album Gish on May 28, 1991.

The Breakout Album

The Smashing Pumpkins release their second album Siamese Dream on July 27, 1993. This is considered to be their breakout album. It debuted at number ten on the Billboard 200 chart. They released four singles , with “Today” and “Disarm” being some of their most popular songs to date. With their popularity continuing to rise, the group go on their Rock Invasion Tour from 1993- 1994. During that tour, they headline Lollapalooza in 1994. In the same year, they release the compilation album Pisces Iscariot, and begin recording their next album.

Mellon Collie And The Infinite Sadness

The band’s third album Mellon Collie And The Infinite Sadness is released on October 24, 1995, and debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart. This album is considered by many to be the Smashing Pumpkins’ best work. Corgan wrote over fifty songs for the album, and eventually had to cut down the number of songs. It is a double album that consists of twenty-eight songs, and is the best-selling double album of the 1990s. The singles “Bullet With Butterfly Wings”, “Tonight, Tonight”, “Zero” and “1979” are also some of their most popular songs today. In 1996, the band goes on their Infinite Sadness Tour, which lasted until 1997. “Bullet With Butterfly Wings” wins the band their first Grammy for “Best Hard Rock Performance” in 1997. They were also nominated for six other Grammys that year.

Adore And Another Grammy

In 1998, The Smashing Pumpkins win their second Grammy for "The End Is The Beginning Is The End" in the “Best Hard Rock Performance” category. After drummer Jimmy Chamberlin departs from the band, Corgan, Iha, and Wretzky release their fourth album Adore on June 2, 1998. It debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 chart. This album has a more electronic sound. Their Adore Tour begins in 1998. In 1999, the group takes a break recording their upcoming album to go on a short club tour called the Arising! Tour. This tour featured the return of Chamberlin and would be the last tour with all four original members.

The End of an Era

Despite Jimmy Chamberlin’s return, D’arcy Wretzky now leaves the band. Her departure comes in the middle of recording the band’s fifth album Machina/The Machines of God. This fifth album is released on February 29, 2000 and debuted at number three on the Billboard 200 chart. For the Sacred and Profane Tour, Melissa Auf der Maur fills in for Wretzky. In May of 2000, Corgan announces that the band with break up at the end of the year. The group does a limited release of their sixth album Machina II/The Friends & Enemies of Modern Music on September 5, 2000. The Smashing Pumpkins play what is often referred to as their “final show” on December 2, 2000 at the Metro in Chicago.

The Return

With the release of the album Zeitgeist in 2007, followed by another break, The Smashing Pumpkins return in 2012. With Corgan being the only original member of the band, they release Oceania on June 19, 2012. Two years later, the band releases their next album Monuments to an Elegy on December 9, 2014.

A Long Awaited Reunion

In 2018, The Smashing Pumpkins announce the return of James Iha and Jimmy Chamberlin. With the band consisting of three out of four of the original members, they release their tenth album Shiny and Oh So Bright Vol. 1 / LP: No Past. No Future. No Sun on November 16, 2018. They begin their Shiny and Oh So Bright Tour in July of 2018. On November 27, 2020, they release their eleventh album CYR.

Billy Corgan And The Smashing Pumpkins Today

Iha and Chamberlin are still in the band with Corgan. The Smashing Pumpkins went on their Rock Invasion 2 Tour in 2022, and the Spirits on Fire Tour with co-headliner Jane’s Addiction in 2022. They also released their most recent album ATUM- Act I & II in 2023, with their single “Beguiled” being very popular. They most recently performed at their The World Is A Vampire Festival in Mexico City, Mexico in 2023. Corgan also has a podcast, called Thirty-Three with William Patrick Corgan, where he discusses the new Smashing Pumpkins album ATUM.
